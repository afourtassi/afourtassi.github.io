I am currently Maître de Conférences (Assistant Professor with tenure) of computer science at Aix-Marseille University, in the NLP group (TALEP).

I am also a Researcher at the Institute of Language, Communication and the Brain (ILCB) — of which I am also a member of the governing board.

Founder of the Computational Communicative Development team (CoCoDev), where we study children's communicative development in natural, interactive, and multimodal contexts.

I have a background in Mathematics and Physics (Ecole Polytechnique), Computational Cognitive Science (Ecole Normale Supérieure), and Experimental Psychology (Stanford University).

Recent Updates:

I will be defending my Habilitation to Direct Research (HDR) on January 17th, titled "How Machine Learning is Transforming the Science of Language Acquisition."

The special issue on "Language Learning, Representation, and Processing in Humans and Machines," which I co-edited with Marianna Apidianaki and Sebastian Padó, has been released in the latest edition Computational Lingusitics (link)

I gave an invited talk at the Mila AI Institute on how AI can be used to study language development in ecological settings (watch the video).
